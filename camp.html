Our Mission

Our Story

From a Borrowed Cabin to 40 Acres of Sacred Ground

Camp Cedarbrook began with a folding table, twelve teenagers, and a borrowed cabin on the Santiam River. Pastor Ruth Osei and youth director Marcus Webb believed that something irreplaceable happens when young people step away from the noise — when they sit around a fire, sing badly, ask hard questions, and find that they are not alone.


Nearly four decades later, that conviction hasn't changed. We've grown to a 40-acre property with eight lodges, a climbing wall, a dock, and a dining hall that seats 200 — but the heart of camp is still a circle of chairs, an honest conversation, and the belief that every camper is known and beloved.

What We Believe

The Values That Shape Everything We Do

✝

Grounded in Scripture

Every program, conversation, and campfire is shaped by the story of a God who pursues and redeems. We take the text seriously — and we take campers' questions just as seriously.

🌲

Creation as Classroom

The woods have always been a place of encounter. We believe getting outside — off screens, into rivers and trees — is itself a spiritual practice, not just a nice add-on.

🤝

Radical Welcome

Cedarbrook is a place where every camper belongs before they've done a single thing to earn it. We work hard to build a community that reflects the breadth of Christ's welcome.

🔥

Honest Faith

We have no interest in a faith that only holds up when life is easy. We make room for doubt, grief, and hard questions — because we believe those are the doorways to deeper belief.

🌱

Whole-Person Formation

Discipleship isn't only about Sunday mornings. We invest in body, mind, and spirit — through challenge courses, creative arts, service projects, and reflective practice.

🏕️

The Long Game

A week at camp isn't the destination — it's a catalyst. We partner with churches and families to make sure what starts at Cedarbrook keeps growing back home.

Our Leadership

The People Behind the Ministry

Our full-time staff bring decades of combined experience in youth ministry, outdoor education, and pastoral care.

RO
Rev. Ruth Osei
Executive Director

Ruth co-founded Cedarbrook in 1987. She holds a D.Min from Fuller Seminary and is an ordained minister in the PCUSA. She still leads cabin devotions every Thursday night.

MW
Marcus Webb
Program Director

Marcus oversees all summer programming and staff training. He spent eight years leading urban youth ministry in Portland before joining Cedarbrook full-time.

JL
Jess Lindqvist
Spiritual Formation

Jess designs the teaching curriculum and trains small-group leaders. She is a graduate of Regent College and an avid backcountry skier.

DM
David Muñoz
Outdoor Education

David is a Wilderness First Responder and Leave No Trace Master Educator. He manages all adventure programming and keeps the climbing wall from falling down.
