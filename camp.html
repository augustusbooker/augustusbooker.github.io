Our Story

From a Madman's Retreat to an Anti-Communist Beacon

The Institute was founded in 1950 by Rev. Kenneth Goff, a former communist and present-day fighter for freedom and God. Goff was labeled a "borderline psychopath" for his anti-communist advocacy, and had suffered bodily injury, but still stood up for what's right by starting a cause he believed in.


The past decade has made the Institute's mission more important than ever. New Federal mandates of racial integration and mental health treatment have tested our nation's founding Christian principles, and communism has continued its unstoppable spread overseas.


Today the Institute is America's greatest bastion of anti-communist teaching. Dozens of students from countless denominations across the country attend each summer to learn from an insider how to face the communist menace.
